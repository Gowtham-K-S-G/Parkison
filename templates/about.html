About Parkinson's Disease

What is Parkinson's Disease?

Parkinson's disease (PD) is a progressive neurodegenerative disorder that affects the central nervous system. It primarily impacts movement and is characterized by both motor and non-motor symptoms.

The disease develops when certain neurons in the brain die or become impaired. These neurons produce dopamine, a chemical that helps control movement. When these neurons die, dopamine levels decrease, leading to the characteristic symptoms of PD.

Common Symptoms

  • Tremors: Shaking, usually in the hands or fingers
  • Bradykinesia: Slowness of movement
  • Rigidity: Stiffness in muscles
  • Postural Instability: Balance problems
  • Micrographia: Small, cramped handwriting

Why Early Detection Matters

Early detection of Parkinson's Disease is crucial because:

Timely Treatment

Early intervention can slow disease progression

Better Quality of Life

Managing symptoms early improves daily living

Care Planning

Families can prepare for future care needs

About This Project

This project implements a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) to analyze spiral drawings for early detection of Parkinson's Disease. The system uses deep learning to identify patterns and abnormalities in drawing that may indicate motor impairment associated with PD.

Key Features:

  • Automated feature learning from spiral images
  • Non-invasive and cost-effective screening method
  • Fast and scalable detection system
  • User-friendly web interface
